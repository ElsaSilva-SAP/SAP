## Part 1: What Are Business Requirements?

**Business requirements** are the **critical activities, needs, and criteria** that an organization must meet to achieve its goals and objectives.

They are used to:
- **Define project scope** ‚Äì what is in and out of the project
- **Develop solutions** ‚Äì what the system must do and how it must perform
- **Ensure business needs are met** ‚Äì so the solution delivers value

Without clear requirements, projects often deliver the wrong thing or miss important needs.

---

## Part 2: Main Types of Requirements

| Type | What it means | Example |
|------|----------------|---------|
| **Functional requirements** | *What* the system must do ‚Äì specific functions and features | ‚ÄúThe system must process sales orders and create invoices.‚Äù |
| **Non-functional requirements** | *How well* the system must perform ‚Äì performance, security, usability, reliability | ‚ÄúThe system must respond within 2 seconds; data must be encrypted.‚Äù |
| **Stakeholder requirements** | Needs of different groups (business users, IT, management) | ‚ÄúFinance users need a monthly closing report; IT needs audit logs.‚Äù |
| **Technical requirements** | Infrastructure and technical specifications | ‚ÄúMust run on SAP HANA; must support 500 concurrent users.‚Äù |

---

## Part 3: Business Requirements in SAP

In SAP projects, the same types apply in a concrete way:

| Type in SAP | What it covers |
|-------------|-----------------|
| **Functional requirements** | Which **SAP modules and functionalities** the system must support (e.g. Finance, HR, Sales, specific reports or workflows). |
| **Non-functional requirements** | **Performance** (response time, throughput), **security** (access, encryption), **usability** (e.g. SAP Fiori), **reliability** (uptime, backup). |
| **Stakeholder requirements** | Needs of **business users** (what they do daily), **IT** (integration, monitoring), **management** (reporting, compliance). |
| **Technical requirements** | **Infrastructure** (servers, database, network), **SAP technical specs** (version, add-ons, interfaces). |

### Think About It

**Why do we need both ‚Äúfunctional‚Äù and ‚Äúnon-functional‚Äù requirements?** *(Hint: one says what the system does; the other says how well it must do it.)*

> Because a system that works functionally but is slow, insecure or unreliable still fails the business.
Both what the system does and how well it performs determine success.

## üéâ Key Takeaways

- **Business requirements** = activities, needs, and criteria to achieve goals; they define scope and guide solution design.
- **Functional** = what the system must do; **non-functional** = how well (performance, security, usability, reliability).
- **Stakeholder** = needs of different groups; **technical** = infrastructure and technical specs.
- In **SAP**, these map to modules/functions, performance/security/usability, user/IT/management needs, and infrastructure.
