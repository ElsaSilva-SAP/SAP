## Part 1: What Is Customer Experience (CX) Design?

**Customer Experience (CX) design** is **creating and optimizing interactions** between a customer and a company to **meet or exceed customer expectations**, **increase satisfaction and loyalty**, and deliver a consistent experience. It covers **all touchpoints** a customer has with the brand (website, app, in-store, support, etc.).

In **business software** (e.g. SAP), ‚Äúcustomers‚Äù are often **internal users** (employees, partners). So CX design here means **user experience (UX)** ‚Äì making systems **easy, clear, and efficient** to use so people adopt them and work better.

---

## Part 2: Components of CX Design

| Component | What it means |
|-----------|----------------|
| **User research** | Understanding user needs, preferences, and behaviors through interviews, surveys, observation |
| **Journey mapping** | Visualizing the user‚Äôs journey across touchpoints and interactions (steps, emotions, pain points) |
| **Service design** | Designing the **services and processes** that support the experience (e.g. how support handles a request) |
| **User interface (UI) design** | Designing the **visual and interactive elements** of digital interfaces (screens, navigation, feedback) |

---

## Part 3: Good vs Poor UX (Business Context)

| Good UX | Poor UX |
|---------|---------|
| Clear labels, few clicks to complete a task | Confusing menus, many steps for simple tasks |
| Consistent layout and behavior across screens | Inconsistent navigation; users get lost |
| Helpful error messages and validation | Cryptic errors; users don‚Äôt know what went wrong |
| Works well on the device users have (e.g. mobile) | Only works on one device or browser |
| Fast response so users aren‚Äôt waiting | Slow screens; users assume the system is broken |

**Why this matters for SAP:** If the **user experience** is poor, people avoid the system, work around it, or make more errors. **SAP Fiori** is SAP‚Äôs approach to a modern, consistent, user-friendly interface ‚Äì better UX leads to better **adoption** and **productivity**.
